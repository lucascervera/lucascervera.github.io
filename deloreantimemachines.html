This page is an example of a Minimum Viable Product , a strategy used for fast and quantitative market testing of a product or product feature, popularized by Eric Ries. A Minimum Viable Product is "that version of a new product which allows a team to collect the maximum amount of validated learning about customers with the least effort". This means that we will not be building the DeLorean Time Machine unless we get a lot of people registered. You know, a time machine is not something you can build in a weekend with lots of coffee. It will take us at least two or three weekends and we will need pizza and beer too...
